Throughout this program, I will help you build solid intuition about the underlying principles of data science

You will understand what data science is, what it is not, and what to expect on the job to avoid common pitfalls due to prior misconceptions.

You will learn about various career paths in data science and analytics, with titles like Data Scientist, Analytics Specialist, Machine Learning Engineer, Product Data Analyst, Data Operations Specialist, etc.

These roles are different from data "analysis", data engineering or BI, and commonly require strong capabilities in statistical thinking, computational thinking, and algorithmic thinking.

Statistical thinking, in that you must be able to discover the intricacies of data using statistics to unravel its distributions, test assumptions about it, and make the right judgements.

Computational thinking, in that you must not only know how to code, but more importantly be able to reason through business or data logic and skillfuly implement this logic in code.

Algorithmic thinking, in that you must not only understand how and when to use an algorithm (computational or statistical), but be able to justify its use, understand its limitations, and evaluate how much risk is plausible based on that limitation.
