Throughout this program, I will help you understand what data science is and what data science is not

This will make you fully informed of what to expect in a data science role and avoid the pitfalls many find themselves in due to prior misconceptions.

For data science jobs particularly, you will need to be in command of a couple of things unlike your data analytics or engineering counterparts, namely strong computational thinking, statistical thinking, and algorithmic thinking.

Computational thinking, in that you must not only know how to code, but more importantly be able to reason through business or data logic and progress through the implementation of this logic in code.

Statistical thinking, in that you must be able to discover the intricacies of data using statistics to unravel its distributions, test assumptions about it, and make the right judgements.

Algorithmic thinking, in that you must not only understand how and when to use a particular algorithm (statistical, analytical or machine learning), but must be able to justify its use, understand its limitations, and determine how much risk is plausible based on that limitation.
